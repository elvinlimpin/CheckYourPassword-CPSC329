    How to create a strong but memorable password

    Us human beings are very inclined to use passwords that contain common dictionary words. This especially applies to English words, mainly because English is a universal language (especially on the internet), which greatly increases the chance of many different users using the same words in their passwords. But this shouldn’t stop you from trying to create good memorable passwords. There are passwords which are easy for humans to remember, but very difficult for computers to crack.

    Click here to reveal some principles that can help with creating memorable but strong passwords.
    1. Keep your password a secret. Please![1,2,3]
    2. Use at least 3 moderate length words that you will remember in your password.[1,3]
      • Try to pick words which others may not be able to easily guess. Keep it personal.
      • You may be able to use sentences, but be careful to ensure that it is a unique and special sentence. (ie. not ILoveYou)
      • Note that this principle by itself is not enough to make a strong password.
    3. If you know multiple languages, use a combination of those languages.[1]
      • Completely made-up words which you can remember are good too.
      • If unique enough, notation from niche domains might also work. Check the examples for what we mean here. [4]
    4. Use numbers, capital characters, and special characters which you won't forget.[1,2,3]
    5. Use different passwords in different websites.[1,2,3]
      • I know this will make remembering passwords tougher, but it’s worth it.
    6. Use two factor authentication if the organization allows so.[1,3]
    7. If you have followed the above suggestions, your password should already be at least 17 characters. Length is strength.[3]
